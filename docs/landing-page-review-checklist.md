# Landing Page Review Checklist

Use this before generating the final implementation and again before deployment.

## Message

- Does the headline explain what LifeOS is in one pass?
- Does the subheadline avoid hype and still feel ambitious?
- Is the page clear about being early, not finished?
- Does the page explain why LifeOS matters beyond "AI on Linux"?

## Truthfulness

- Are all claims defendable today?
- Are there any implied promises that sound more complete than reality?
- Does "What Works Today" describe foundations, not fantasy?
- Are there fake numbers, fake proof, or implied GA availability?

## Audience Fit

- Would a grant reviewer understand the mission quickly?
- Would an open-source contributor understand why the project is interesting?
- Would a privacy-conscious user understand the differentiation?
- Would a potential sponsor see seriousness and direction?

## Design

- Does it clearly look like LifeOS and not like a fresh unrelated brand?
- Does it look like an operating system project, not a SaaS dashboard?
- Is the palette anchored in teal, not random neon?
- Is teal the main accent and pink a restrained secondary accent?
- Did we avoid drifting into blue-first, purple-first, or generic startup styling?
- Is pink used sparingly and intentionally?
- Does the layout feel deliberate on desktop and mobile?
- Is there enough visual atmosphere without losing readability?
- If a mascot or illustration appears, does it respect the existing Axi identity instead of reinventing it?

## CTA Quality

- Are the main CTAs still only:
  - Get updates
  - View on GitHub
  - Support LifeOS
- Is the CTA hierarchy obvious?
- Does every CTA lead somewhere meaningful?

## Sections

- Hero
- Why LifeOS
- What Works Today
- Why This Matters
- Road to Public Beta
- Follow the Project
- Support LifeOS
- Footer

## Product Positioning

- Does the page clearly communicate local-first AI?
- Does it clearly communicate privacy by default?
- Does it clearly communicate AI-native OS instead of "AI app"?
- Does it show sovereignty and ownership as the deeper thesis?

## Accessibility

- Is contrast good enough?
- Are focus states obvious?
- Is the typography readable?
- Is reduced motion respected?

## Final Gut Check

- Does this feel real?
- Does this feel like LifeOS?
- Does this feel like the current LifeOS brand made public, instead of a totally different website identity?
- Would this make the right person want to follow the project?
